People often search for an otoneurologist when they feel dizzy or have vertigo. Here is when a neurologist can help and when ENT may be needed.

Many people search for an “otoneurologist” when they have dizziness, vertigo or balance problems. It is understandable, because dizziness can come from the inner ear, but it can also come from the nervous system.

Revimed PLUS+ does not present itself as an ENT centre. But a neurological consultation can be useful when dizziness repeats, appears with headaches, numbness, weakness, unstable walking or the feeling that you are losing control.

A neurologist can check whether the symptoms seem to come from the nervous system. If the problem looks more connected to the inner ear, the patient may also be guided to an ENT specialist.

Why is dizziness not treated the same way in everyone?

Because dizziness can have different causes. In one patient it may be neck tension. In another, the inner ear. In another, anxiety, fatigue, blood pressure or the effect of medication.

This is why it is important not to choose treatment randomly. First, the doctor needs to understand where the problem may be coming from.

What should you tell the doctor?

Tell the doctor if dizziness appears when you turn your head, stand up, after stress, after effort or after certain medication. Mention nausea, noise in the ears, hearing changes, double vision, headache, numbness or weakness.

These details help a lot. Sometimes a small detail changes the direction of the evaluation.

What treatment can be recommended?

It depends on the case. Sometimes exercises, physiotherapy, procedures and simple routine changes help. Sometimes medication is needed. Sometimes the best option is a combination.

At Revimed PLUS+, the doctor discusses options with the patient. If the patient prefers a gentler approach and the case allows it, that can be tried. If medication is needed, the reason is explained and tolerance is monitored.

If something does not suit the patient, the plan can be adjusted.

Experience and individual approach

Revimed PLUS+ is a clinic founded in 2005. Our doctor has more than 35 years of experience in the field. With symptoms like dizziness, experience matters because there is rarely only one possible cause.

The goal is to understand the specific case and choose a realistic option for the patient: medical treatment, physiotherapy, gentler methods or referral to another specialist if needed.
