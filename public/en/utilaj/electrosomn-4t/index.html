Electrosleep therapy

Electrosleep-4T

Portable device for electrosleep therapy.

What electrosleep therapy is

  • Electrosleep therapy is a neurotropic physiotherapy method based on the effect of low-frequency, low-intensity pulsed current on the central nervous system.
  • The method received its name because many patients experience deep relaxation, drowsiness or sleep during the procedure.
  • The therapy is aimed at functional regulation of cortical and subcortical structures involved in nervous, autonomic and endocrine regulation.

Possible therapeutic effects

  • reduction of emotional excitability;
  • sedative and anti-stress effect;
  • support of natural sleep quality;
  • reduction of fatigue;
  • support of autonomic and functional balance.

How the procedure is performed

  • The procedure is usually performed in a quiet, comfortable room with reduced light and a relaxed patient position.
  • Special electrodes are applied according to ophthalmic-retromastoid or frontal-retromastoid methods.
  • Session duration and treatment course are selected individually by the physician.

Contraindications and precautions

  • individual intolerance to electrical current;
  • inflammatory eye diseases;
  • facial dermatitis;
  • retinal detachment, closed-angle glaucoma, severe myopia;
  • advanced cataract;
  • some psychiatric or neurological conditions and general contraindications to physiotherapy.
